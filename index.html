A simple introduction to the coral's matters and why you should care :

02

Why should you care? Simple, corals are the heart of our reefs, they keep the balance in this ecosystem. Our corals are dying a rapid past for the past years, in fact from 2009 to 2018, our earth lost 14% of its global coral on reefs, which equal as 11 700 sKm. It is said to be more then all the living coral in Australia, which is the sixth largest country in the world.

The consequences of overfishing, pollution and climate change are affecting the temperature and its natural chimical, causing our coral to “bleach”.

Coral reefs are not only beneficial for their fellow sea creatures but also for us! Yes they are a key component to fishes but they are also providing us food, protection and for medicine. The reefs protect coastline from storms and erosion (erosion being the natural process of wind or water to gradually worn and dig earthen materials through years) which secure millions of people living on the coast.

There is about 6 000 known coral species out there, knowing that we only know about 5% of our planet oceans. Each coral system can host over 4 000 other species of fishes and aquatic cnidarians, molluscs and crustaceans.There is much more to learn about these beautiful systems.

Global Coral Bleaching

The first increase of mass coral bleaching first occured in 1998, documenting around 14% of Earth's coral population since 2009.

A hopefull future

Even with the drastic bleaching events, in 2019 the amount of coral on earth increared by 2%. Scientits discovered coral habitats that are more resilient to climate changes.

Welcome to the Baltic seaside

The Baltic Sea is a mediterranean sea of the Atlantic Ocean, enclosed by Denmark, Estonia, Finland, Latvia, Lithuania, Sweden, northeast Germany, Poland, Russia and the North and Central European Plain. The sea stretches from 53°N to 66°N latitude and from 10°E to 30°E longitude.
